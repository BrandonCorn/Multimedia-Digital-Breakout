 On their way through Tennessee, the gang makes their next stop by the Nolichucky River to pick up “good buddy” Nick. He’s known as “good buddy” Nick because he calls everyone good buddy. After picking him up everyone decides they’re starving and need some food asap. Good buddy Nick mentions the go-to must-have place that you can’t get anywhere else is Pal’s. Pal’s is a drive-thru burger joint that only has locations in Tennessee. Everyone agrees on the place and off they go.

When they arrive...

at Pal’s they notice there is an extremely long line, or queue. This is the perfect time to introduce the next data structure, Queues. A Queue is a first-in first-out structure that maintains a list of objects, adding and removing them in a specific way. When we add objects to a Queue, we “queue” them, which means to add them to the end of the list. To remove objects from a Queue, we “dequeue” them, which is to remove the objects at the front of the list.

Our place...

in the drive-thru is a perfect example of how a Queue works. By entering the drive-thru, we have queued ourselves, or joined the line of cars at the end of the line. Each time the car at the front of the line receives their order, they are dequeued, meaning they are leaving the line. Each time a car leaves the line, the car after that becomes the new front of the line. Just like normal lines in places like this or the grocery store, you can’t enter the line in any position. Also, like waiting in a normal line you can’t just choose to cut in front of whoever is in front of you, they must be tended to first and you must wait until you are at the front.
